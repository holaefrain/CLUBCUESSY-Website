CLUBCUESSY

STANDARDS OF MOVEMENT

Rite of Dance

Our Rite of Dance recognizes that dancing is not just a physical act, rather it’s a declaration of freedom, a rebellion against self-consciousness, and a way to reconnect with true joy.

It’s not about performance. It’s about presence.

CLUBCUESSY recognizes that music is not a product of ego, but a shared emotional language. By removing identity from the equation, the audience is invited to listen more deeply, feel more freely, and connect more authentically with the sound, the space, and themselves.

Law of Liberation

The Law of Liberation declares dance floors shall be considered judgment-free zones, where individuals may move, express, and exist without fear of ridicule, surveillance, or unsolicited critique.

We aim to protect the emotional freedom, dignity, and creative expression of individuals in communal dance spaces by outlawing judgmental behavior and promoting radical acceptance.

No Camera Policy

CLUBCUESSY aims to preserve the sanctity of live music environments by prohibiting the use of cameras, both photography and videography, and unauthorized digital documentation within designated venues. This policy aims to foster uninhibited self-expression, protect communal intimacy, and restore presence to the music experience.

Upon entry, attendees must apply tamper-proof lens tape provided by the venue.

Refusal to comply may result in denied entry or removal.

Anonymous Performance Act

The Anonymous Performance Act (APA) attempts to preserve the sanctity of musical expression by removing artist identity and ego from promotional materials and event announcements, encouraging audiences to engage with music without bias, expectation, or distraction. CLUBCUESSY strives to create an experience you will never forget.

Have trust in us as we give you our best.

Artists may choose to reveal their identity after the event concludes.
